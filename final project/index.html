Fried Chicken Trivia

When did fried chicken first appear in the United States?

Fried chicken first appeared in the United States during the 18th century. It is believed to have been introduced by Scottish immigrants who brought their tradition of deep-frying chicken in fat or oil to America. Over time, the dish evolved and became popular in the Southern states, where it gained its iconic status. Southern-style fried chicken is typically seasoned with a variety of spices, coated in flour or batter, and then deep-fried to a crispy golden brown. It has since become a beloved classic in American cuisine, often served with sides like biscuits, coleslaw, and mashed potatoes.

(Source: Oregonlive)
